# Delivery and Logistics Procedures

### Delivery and Logistics Procedures for QuickBite

These procedures outline how **QuickBite** manages the delivery process from order acceptance to successful handover. They are user-facing guides to set expectations, explain statuses, and provide clear steps for troubleshooting common delivery-related issues. QuickBite partners with independent delivery partners (gig workers) who use our app for routing, navigation, and communication.

Delivery times vary based on distance, traffic, weather, restaurant preparation speed, and demand. Typical estimates: 30–60 minutes from placement to delivery in urban areas like Delhi.

**General Reporting Process**:

1. Open QuickBite app > "Orders" > Select active/completed order.
2. Tap "Help" or "Report Issue."
3. Choose category (e.g., "Delivery Problem") > Add details/photos.
4. Submit—AI reviews instantly; human escalation if needed (response <5 min typically).
5. Track resolution in notifications/email.

**Prevention Tips**:

- Enable precise location and notifications.
- Add clear delivery instructions (e.g., "Gate code 1234", "Leave at doorstep").
- Avoid changing address mid-delivery (causes rerouting delays).
- During monsoons or peak hours (e.g., weekends in Pitampura), add buffer time or choose scheduled delivery.

#### 2. Driver Communication Protocols

QuickBite prioritizes safe, masked communication between users and delivery partners.

**How to Contact Your Delivery Partner**:

- **In-App Chat**: Available once "Out for Delivery." Use for quick questions (e.g., "Please call when near gate").
- **Masked Call**: Tap phone icon—connects without sharing real numbers (privacy protected).
- **Best Practices**:
  - Be polite and clear.
  - Avoid sharing personal details.
  - Use only for delivery-related issues (e.g., access, location clarification).
  - If harassment or unsafe behavior, report immediately via "Help" > "Partner Issue"—QuickBite investigates and may deactivate partner.

**What Delivery Partners Can/Cannot Do**:

- Can: Ask for gate codes, confirm exact spot, provide ETA.
- Cannot: Enter your home without permission, accept cash tips outside app, or make personal requests.

#### 3. Weather and External Disruption Policies

External factors can impact deliveries—QuickBite adjusts dynamically.

**Key Guidelines**:

- **Weather Alerts**: App shows warnings (e.g., heavy rain in Delhi NCR). Partners may pause or slow down for safety.
- **Surge in Delays**: High demand or events trigger longer ETAs and possible surge fees.
- **Force Majeure Events**: Strikes, natural disasters, or city-wide issues may lead to temporary service suspension.
- **User Options During Disruptions**:
  - Switch to pickup (if available—no delivery fee).
  - Cancel before "Preparing" for full refund.
  - If already en route, expect delays but possible partial credits.

**Compensation for Disruptions**:

- No automatic refunds for weather/traffic (uncontrollable).
- If delay >90 minutes due to partner fault, goodwill credit offered.
- Safety first: Partners may cancel if unsafe (e.g., flooding)—full refund issued.

These procedures ensure transparent, efficient logistics for QuickBite users. Our goal is reliable delivery with minimal hassle. For real-time tracking, always rely on the app's live map and notifications. If you encounter persistent issues or need region-specific adjustments (e.g., for Delhi traffic patterns), reach out via in-app support.
